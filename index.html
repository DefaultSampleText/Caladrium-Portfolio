Section I

Emerging Design Identity

When I began Game Development, I didn’t have much of a path set out for myself; I figured I liked doing creative things, and making games pretty sounded interesting, so I just went for it. It was actually a very close choice between doing this for my degree and focusing on Filmmaking instead. And though I may go back to studying film at a later date, I am really glad I chose game dev. During the short time I’ve been taking this course, I've already found myself doing so many amazing new things. I’ve made art that I was always too scared to give a try at, I’ve made connections with peers in my course at a time when I found myself socially withdrawn, and it's all been amazing.

A lot of my focus so far has not been as much on the game creation side, but rather the artistic and graphic design portion of the course. But I hope to continue to explore all the paths ahead of me so I can experience every part of being a designer.

“And now I see…”

Section II

Learning and Skills Snapshot

A quick overview of the tools, methods, and design habits I have begun developing.

Visual Design

I have become a lot more confident in composing graphics through this course. While I already had some knowledge of the basic components of art, I have definitely deepened my understanding. Each piece I produce is made with stronger layouts, better-fitting colours, and higher quality imagery; I’m learning how to make memorable art.

Digital Tools

While I have done work in similarly styled programs, having full access to the Adobe suite has been an amazing boon in studying this course. Being able to use Photoshop has really levelled up my ability to make striking imagery with all the tools it provides. I have also been learning HTML and CSS, which has given me the skills to build websites like this.

Research & Reflection

One of the biggest skills I have started developing is the ability to look back on my own work and understand what I was trying to achieve, what succeeded, and what could be improved. Earlier in the course, I often saw design as simply making something look good, but I am beginning to understand how much planning, intention, audience awareness, and reflection sits behind strong visual work. I want to keep improving at documenting my process, explaining my choices clearly, and using feedback to make my work stronger.

“With eye serene…”

Section III

Discipline or Stream Direction

While I’ll continue my focus on game development going forward, I am starting to find more love for the idea of continuing to learn more about graphic design and visual arts, and potentially following a separate artistic path alongside my plans to work in game development.

As for my future directive within game dev, I’m already starting plans to get ahead. I recently joined a small game development team that is looking to grow into an established studio in Canberra, so before I’ve even finished my course, I’ll have experience in the industry.

Section IV

Study Pathway and Unit Choices

Future units I am interested in taking and how they may shape my degree pathway.

01

Introduction to Creative Writing

I’ve generally found that world writing is one of my strongest skills, so I’m looking forward to developing my skills in that field and learning more about how to write engagingly.

02

FProgramming for Design

I’m really interested in furthering my coding skills, as it is something I’ve sought to improve for a long time. I have gotten to dip my toes into the subject with IxD, but I hope to become a proficient coder.

03

Tabletop Game Design

I am interested in taking Tabletop Game Design because it connects directly to my love of games, storytelling, worldbuilding, and player experience. While video games are my main area of study, I hope this unit will help me better understand how systems can create meaningful experiences for players, and how design choices can support imagination, collaboration, and replayability.

“The very pulse of the machine.”

Section V

Early Career and Professional Outlook

At this stage, I am most interested in creative roles connected to game development, visual design, and worldbuilding. I am not completely locked into one specific job title yet, but I can see myself moving toward areas such as concept art, visual design, narrative design, UI design, or broader creative work within a small game development team. What appeals to me about these areas is the way they combine practical design decisions with imagination. I enjoy making work that feels atmospheric and memorable, but I also like thinking about how creative choices shape a person's experience of a game or story.

My interest in game development has also become more realistic since beginning this course. At first, I mostly thought about games through the lens of art and aesthetics, but I am beginning to understand that design work also involves communication, planning, iteration, teamwork, and responsibility. Working professionally means being able to explain my decisions, respond to feedback, meet deadlines, and create work that serves a purpose beyond my own taste. Since I have recently joined a small game development team that hopes to grow into an established studio in Canberra, I also have an opportunity to begin learning these professional habits in a real collaborative environment.

My realistic next steps are to keep developing my visual portfolio, improve my coding skills, and gain more experience working with others on creative projects. I want to continue learning tools like Photoshop, Illustrator, HTML, CSS, and game development software, while also strengthening my writing and design reflection skills. During my degree, I hope to keep building projects that show both my artistic style and my ability to think like a designer.
